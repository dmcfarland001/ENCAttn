    Attention and Distraction
    The Concept

    Have you ever thought, and we mean really thought about your use of attention? How about the lack of control you have over it? Scary stuff, we know. This web page is dedicated to bringing awareness on how our attention has been affected by the advent of new technologies. The smart phone, laptop, applications and notifications have all provided us with a slew of distractions from our day-to-day lives.

    According to scholar Howard Rheingold, “you don't have to work to make your mind wander” (Rheingold 38). This fact is present through the constant use of our devices. We tend to reach for our technologies at the sight of any potential engagement. A ring, buzz, blink and vibration all set us off course and into the realm of being digitally distracted. Though this isn't always entirely bad, consider this: according to an article in the Harvard Business Review entitled Conquering Digital Distraction, a recent study found that distracted workers cost the economy an average of $995 billion a year (Rosen, Samuel 111). This high rate of distraction can be attributed to what Rheingold calls “attentional blink”. That is, the temporary disruption caused when attention shifts from one thing to another. With 11 million bits of information interacting with us every second and only 40 of those being processed consciously, it can get hard to prioritize focus over distraction (Rheingold 39). Research suggests that this information overload is the cause for much of our what leads us to be distracted.

    In another study, German researchers who examined device-using students, found that “attention is a mental mechanism that can be focused on stimuli in order to process them, but only a limited quantity of stimuli can be processed at any given time. If demands exceed available attentional capacity, the cognitive system overloads and performance suffers” (Aagaard 6). In other words, we can be overwhelmed and compromised by the amount of stimuli we encounter.

    What then is the root of our distraction, how is it affecting us and what do we do about it? Well, this page aims to find out just that.
