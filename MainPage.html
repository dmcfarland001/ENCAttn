    The Concept

    Have you ever thought, and we mean really thought about your use of attention? How about the lack of control you have over it? Scary stuff, we know. This web page is dedicated to bringing awareness on how our attention has been affected by the advent of new technologies. The smart phone, laptop, applications and notifications have all provided us with a slew of distractions from our day-to-day lives.

    According to scholar Howard Rheingold, "you don't have to work to make your mind wander" (PAGE). This fact is present through the constant use of our devices. We tend to reach for our technologies at the sight of any potential engagement. A ring, buzz, blink and vibration all set us off course and into the realm of being digitally distracted. Though this isn't always entirely bad, consider this: according to an article in the Harvard Business Review entitled Conquering Digital Distraction, a recent study found that distracted workers cost the economy an average of $995 billion a year (Conquering Digital Distraction 111). This high rate of distraction can be attributed to what Rheingold calls "attentional blink". That is, the temporary disruption caused when attention shifts from one thing to another. With 11 million bits of information interacting with us every second and only 40 of those being processed consciously, it can get hard to prioritize focus over distraction (PAGE RHEINGOLD). Research suggests that this information overload is the cause for much of our what leads us to be distracted.

    In another study, German researchers who studied device-using students, found that device-using students researchers found that "attention is a mental mechanism that can be focused on stimuli in order to process them, but only a limited quantity of stimuli can be processed at any given time. If demands exceed available attentional capacity, the cognitive system overloads and performance suffers" (Aagaard PAGE). In other words, we can be overwhelmed and compromised by the amount of stimuli we encounter.

    What then is the root of our distraction, how is it affecting us and what do we do about it? Well, this page aims to find out just that.

    First and foremost, there was a degree of meta thinking that went into creating this page. Particularly because the topic had to deal with attention and distraction. What was ironic at least for a few of us, was the fact that even though we were researching and were aware of the problem of attention/distraction, we could not quickly change our digital habits. Knowing full well of all of the possible consequences, we continued our daily routine of checking our phone, playing games, logging on to social media, and browsing content in general. That was the most telling aspect of this project, how difficult it is to �wean� ourselves off of our personal media devices, even with the facts right in our faces. In this sense, the lesson was that this topic is both important and applicable to the students that will be visiting our page. As we truly began to understand what attention and distraction can do to how we function as human beings, it became prevalent that everything that distracts/attracts us is deliberately done. The power lies with the companies and people who create this content, not with the audience. It is only when the audience becomes aware of what is going on around them, that they can truly begin to regain that power. It is essentially a sense of digital literacy; being able to communicate, decipher and download information in an environment where social and philosophical trends are changing by the second. As Yoram Eshet-Alkalai and Oren Soffer point out, Professors at The Open University of Israel, �digital technologies have penetrated almost every aspect of our lives.� It is for this reason that they argue, and so do we, that users need to master an array of social and cognitive competencies that allow us to function effectively online. It is our intention for this webpage to serve as one of the foundational resources for the course. It helps set the basis for understanding online behavior and why it has a hold on us. Something that this page will not provide is a step-by-step guide. What this page will provide rather, is a way of thinking and processing information, to where our audience will be able to reach a solution naturally. We hope our page serves as a lense in which to view human interactions in a digital space. Being that this is a course all about digital environments we feel that the cause, effect and solution to distraction are the perfect way to introduce the content the class will uncover.
